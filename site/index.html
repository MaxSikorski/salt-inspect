How It Works

SALT learns what "normal" looks like, then flags anything that deviates. No labeled defect images required.

1

Learn Normal

Stage 1 trains a vision model on thousands of normal parts using masked image reconstruction. The model learns the structure and appearance of defect-free components.

2

Build Representations

Stage 2 freezes the teacher and trains a student model using JEPA latent prediction. The student learns robust, compact representations of normal parts — no EMA needed.

3

Detect Anomalies

At inference, the model encodes a new image into 196 patch embeddings. Each patch is compared against normal references — patches that deviate are flagged as potential defects.

Architecture
Vision Transformer 224×224 input 16×16 patches 14×14 grid 192-dim embeddings 12 transformer layers

Training Results

Trained on MVTec AD — the industry-standard benchmark for industrial anomaly detection. 15 categories of manufactured parts, trained on normal images only.

Stage 1: MAE Reconstruction Loss

Pixel-level reconstruction of masked patches — the teacher learns visual structure.

Stage 2: JEPA Latent Loss

Student predicts frozen teacher representations — builds compact defect-sensitive features.

Where This Applies

Self-supervised defect detection eliminates the need for thousands of labeled defect images. Train on normal parts, deploy on the edge.

3D Printing QC

Detect layer shifts, warping, stringing, and under-extrusion on printed parts in real-time during production.

Electronics Inspection

PCB solder joint verification, component placement validation, and surface defect detection on circuit boards.

CNC & Machining

Surface finish verification, dimensional accuracy checks, and tool wear detection on machined metal components.

Textile & Materials

Fabric defect detection, surface contamination screening, and material consistency verification across production runs.

Runs on the Edge

The ONNX model is under 20MB and runs in-browser or on edge devices. No cloud dependency, no data leaves the factory floor. Deploy on Raspberry Pi, Jetson, or any device with a browser.
